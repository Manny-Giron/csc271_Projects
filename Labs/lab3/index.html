Why I love burritos

   The burrito is debatebly my most favorite dish for a few reasons. Firstly, I find it delicious.
After that, I like how customizable it is. You can add or remove ingredients to fit your taste.
And, its a dish that has pretty much everything you need in one meal. It has carbs, protein, and vegetables.
It is quite filling, sometimes too filling.

    Interestingly, the name Burrito actually means "little donkey" in Spanish due to the belief that the 
food resembles a donkey's pack

What makes a burrito?

    The burrito is fundamentally comprised of a soft flour tortilla that wraps around a variety of fillings. 
At its core, a burrito is defined by its portability and the way its ingredients are enclosed, making it easy to eat on the go. 
While the specific fillings can vary widely, the essential characteristic is the combination of ingredients all wrapped together, 
creating a balanced and satisfying meal in a single package.

General Instructions:

  1. Warm the tortilla in a skillet or microwave to make it pliable.
  2. Lay the tortilla flat and layer the ingredients in the center (start with rice or potatoes, then protein, beans, cheese, veggies, and toppings).
  3. Fold the sides of the tortilla over the filling, then roll it up tightly from the bottom.
  4. Serve immediately, or wrap in foil to keep warm.
